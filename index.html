💡 OVERNIGHT STOP TIPS

Fridge is your biggest draw

At 60W × 15hrs overnight = 75Ah alone. It's always on regardless of what else you do.

Coffee maker in the morning

900W × 25min = ~38Ah through the inverter. Big hit but worth it — just know it's coming.

Furnace blower vs. fan

Cold night? Propane does the heat, the 12V blower is cheap (~4Ah). Hot night? A fan costs ~23Ah but beats the A/C.

Slides & leveling are one-time

Only ~5Ah total for arrive + depart. Don't sweat these — they're tiny.

📐 CALCULATION REFERENCE
STEP 1 — WATT-HOURS PER PHASE
Wh = Watts × Hours
Calculated separately for Evening and Morning hours, then summed for Total.
e.g. Coffee Maker: 900W × 0.4 hrs = 360 Wh
STEP 2A — 12V DC ITEMS (direct)
Ah = Wh ÷ 12V
12V items draw directly from the battery with no conversion loss.
e.g. Fridge: 60W × 15 hrs = 900 Wh ÷ 12 = 75.0 Ah
STEP 2B — 120V AC ITEMS (inverter)
Ah = (Wh ÷ 0.88) ÷ 12V
120V items run through the inverter at 88% efficiency — the battery must supply ~14% extra to cover the conversion loss.
e.g. Coffee Maker: (360 Wh ÷ 0.88) ÷ 12 = 34.1 Ah
STEP 3 — USABLE BATTERY CAPACITY
Usable Ah = Bank Size × Depth of Discharge
You can't safely use 100% of a battery. Depth of discharge (DoD) limits how far you drain it.
LiFePO4: 95% DoD  ·  AGM: 50% DoD  ·  Flooded: 50% DoD
STEP 4 — BATTERY SUFFICIENCY CHECK
% Used = Total Ah ÷ Usable Ah × 100
Green ≤ 70%  ·  Yellow 70–90%  ·  Red > 90%
Recommended bank = Total Ah × 1.15, rounded to nearest 50Ah (15% safety buffer)
BONUS — MINIMUM INVERTER SIZE
Inverter W ≥ Peak Single Load (W)
Your inverter must handle the highest single AC wattage in your list. Size up 20–25% for headroom and surge capacity on motor loads.
e.g. Hair dryer at 1,800W → minimum 2,000W inverter
